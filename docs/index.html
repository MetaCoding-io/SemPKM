Why SemPKM

PKM tools force a tradeoff. We don't.

Existing tools make you choose between flexibility and structure. SemPKM gives you both.

The problem

Markdown-first tools are flexible and portable, but structure is implicit and semantics are informal. Object-first tools offer structure and views, but lock you into a platform with no interoperability.

  • Informal structure means no reliable queries or views
  • Platform lock-in means your data isn't truly yours
  • No standard format means fragile integrations

The SemPKM answer

A standards-native semantic datastore with an IDE-grade UX. Your knowledge lives as typed, queryable, interoperable data — and you own all of it.

  • RDF + SPARQL for powerful, reliable queries
  • SHACL shapes drive forms, validation, and linting
  • Export as JSON-LD, project to Obsidian, publish via SOLID
  • Mental Models: install a bundle, get an instant workflow
Features

Everything you need to think in structure

SemPKM combines the power of semantic web standards with an interface designed for humans.

Mental Models

Installable domain bundles — ontology, shapes, views, and projections — that give you an instant, structured PKM experience. No blank-page syndrome.

SHACL-Driven Forms

Forms are auto-generated from SHACL shapes. Constraints drive validation, linting, and layout hints — no custom UI code needed.

Typed Relationships

Edges are first-class: clickable, annotatable, and versionable. Explore your knowledge as a web of typed, meaningful connections.

Graph Visualization

Semantic-aware graph views with type-based styling, predicate-driven edges, and multiple layouts — force-directed, tree, and radial.

SPARQL Console & Views

Tables, cards, and graph views backed by SPARQL queries. Plus a built-in interactive SPARQL console for ad-hoc exploration of your data.

Spatial Canvas

Drag and drop objects onto an infinite spatial canvas. Arrange your knowledge visually, connect ideas freely, and think in two dimensions.

Command Palette

Press Ctrl+K to launch the command palette. Quickly navigate objects, switch views, and trigger actions — all from the keyboard.

SHACL Lint & Validation

Live SHACL validation on every object. A dedicated lint dashboard shows conformance status at a glance — catch constraint violations instantly.

Inference Engine

Define rules in your Mental Models and let SemPKM infer new relationships automatically. Derived edges are tagged as inferred for full transparency.

Standards-Based, No Lock-In

Built on RDF, JSON-LD, SPARQL, and SHACL. Export anything. Project your graph as files for Obsidian. Publish via SOLID and ActivityPub.

Webhook Automations

Configure outbound webhooks that fire on object changes, edge updates, or validation events. Filter by type and integrate with any external service.

WebID & IndieAuth

Decentralized identity built in. Create a WebID profile, authenticate with IndieAuth, and manage authorized apps — no centralized auth provider needed.

LLM Integration

Connect your own LLM for AI-powered features. Configure the connection in settings and get intelligent assistance across your knowledge graph.

Event Sourcing & Audit Trail

Every change is an immutable event. The current state is a derived projection. Full history, complete auditability, automation-ready.

Self-Hosted, Own Your Data

Run SemPKM on your own hardware. Single Docker command. Your knowledge graph stays on your machine — no cloud required.

And More Coming Soon

ActivityPub publishing, Mental Model marketplace, collaborative editing, and much more on the roadmap.

Obsidian Import

Bring your Obsidian vault with you

Already have an Obsidian vault? SemPKM's built-in import wizard converts your markdown notes into typed, queryable knowledge objects in six guided steps.

1

Upload Your Vault

Drag and drop your vault as a ZIP file. SemPKM detects and resumes previous in-progress imports automatically.

2

Scan & Analyze

Live scanning with real-time progress. Detects note types from frontmatter, folder names, and tags. Shows stats, warnings, and detected groups.

3

Map Types

Map each detected group to a Mental Model type — Person, Project, Note, Concept — or skip groups you don't need.

4

Map Properties

Frontmatter keys are matched to RDF properties from your SHACL shapes. Custom IRI support for anything that doesn't map cleanly.

5

Preview

See sample objects with their mapped properties before committing. Verify everything looks right before the import runs.

6

Import & Resolve

Two-pass import: first creates typed objects, then resolves [[wiki-links]] into RDF edges. Full progress log and error reporting.

Smart Type Detection

Frontmatter fields, folder structure, and tags are analyzed in priority order to automatically categorize your notes.

Wiki-Link Resolution

[[links]] become typed dcterms:references edges. Aliases are preserved. Unresolved targets are reported.

Safe Re-Import

Each imported note is tracked via sempkm:importSource. Re-importing a vault skips duplicates automatically.
